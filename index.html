Scoring Methodology

PCA-derived weights, confidence-adjusted scoring, Monte Carlo uncertainty — statistically defensible and academically auditable.

45%
Physical risk
  • Flood risk index (ISRO Bhuvan + SRTM DEM)
  • Rainfall intensity (IMD + CHIRPS v2)
  • Urban heat island (MODIS LST + Landsat)
  • Water stress (CGWB + BWSSB)
30%
Exposure risk
  • Population density (Census + WorldPop)
  • Built-up fraction (ISRO LISS-IV 5.8m)
  • Infrastructure vulnerability (BBMP+BESCOM)
  • Drainage capacity (BBMP SWD GIS atlas)
25%
Financial risk
  • Property price volatility (IGR Karnataka)
  • Insurance claim proxy (IRDAI district)
  • Loan default proxy (CIBIL PIN-code)
  • Tanker cost burden (BWSSB dispatch)

PCA-derived weights

Layer weights (0.45/0.30/0.25) derived from PCA on a 25×12 normalised ward matrix using scikit-learn. PC1 explains 82.6% of variance — statistically confirming the composite index is valid. No hand-picking.

Monte Carlo uncertainty

1,000 simulations per ward with Gaussian noise scaled to confidence. Average CI width: 3–4 CRCS points. Borderline wards (CI crossing a band boundary) are flagged for manual underwriter review.
